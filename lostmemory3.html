Day by day, I learned to navigate this new existence. I created new memories with my family, even if the old ones remained elusive. Emma and I started going to therapy together, and slowly, we rebuilt our life on the fragile foundation of my forgotten past.

There are moments when I feel the weight of my loss—the loss of my parents, my memories, my sense of self. But I also feel the strength of the love that surrounds me, a love that survived even when I couldn't remember it. Emma, Lily, and Max remind me every day that while the past may be lost, the present and the future are ours to shape.

In the quiet moments of the night, when the house is still and the world outside fades, I sometimes catch a glimpse of my parents in my dreams. They smile at me, as if to say it's okay, as if to reassure me that they are at peace. And perhaps, in those fleeting moments, I find a piece of the Jason I once was.
